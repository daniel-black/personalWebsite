Some of my all-time favorites:

  • Zen and the Art of Motorcyle Maintenance

    The first time I read this book I sped through it and totally missed the entire point. Take your time with this book, enjoy it like a slow motorcyle ride across the country.

  • Zero to One

    This is my most re-read book by a long shot. If I had to boil it down to three words, it would be Do New Things. Posed on the opening page is the famous contrarian question:

    "What important truth do very few people agree with you on?"

    Good answers are hard to find but just being aware of the question helps to keep your eyes open.

  • Last and First Men

    An amazingly weird and expansive sci-fi novel from 1930 that follows humanity deep into the future all the way up to her last days. What more could you want?

  • The Sovereign Individual

    This book was ahead of it's time. One of my biggest takeaways is that different technologies raise or lower the returns to violence. The classic example is encryption. There is no amount of tanks, guns, and missiles that you can throw at a modern encryption algorithm to make the math bend to your will. And that is very cool.

  • The Wright Brothers

    This book makes you feel like you can put a dent in the universe. No one had flown. People didn't fly. These boys just put their heads down and built those flying machines. Wild.

  • Anna Karenina

    This is just a beautifully human story in all of our messiness and I love it. Read it please.

  • Fooled by Randomness

    A lot to learn from this one. Numbers, man. They can really get ya sometimes.

  • Dominion

    Really thorough examination and history of the motive force shaping life in the West for the last 2000 years.
